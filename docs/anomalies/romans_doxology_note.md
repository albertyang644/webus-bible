# romans_doxology_note.md

## Overview

The doxology traditionally associated with **Romans 16:25–27** appears in this dataset at a different location:

* **Romans 14:26**

This is **intentional** and reflects a known textual tradition rather than a data error.

---

## What is the Doxology?

The doxology is the closing praise section beginning with:

> “Now to him who is able…”
> or
> “to the only wise God…”

It is a standard concluding element in the Epistle to the Romans.

---

## Placement Variation

Across manuscript traditions, the doxology appears in different locations:

1. At the end of **Romans 16** (most modern Bibles)
2. At the end of **Romans 14** (**this dataset**)
3. In both locations (less common duplication cases)

---

## Behavior in This Dataset

* **Romans 14:26** → contains the doxology text ✅
* **Romans 16:25** → present as an empty placeholder ⚠
* **Romans 16:26–27** → not present ❌

This indicates that the doxology has been **relocated**, not removed.

---

## Interpretation

This is:

* **Not a missing verse issue**
* **Not a parsing or ingestion error**
* **Not corruption**

It is:

> **A valid textual placement variation preserved from the source tradition**

---

## Handling Guidance

Do **not**:

* Move the doxology to Romans 16
* Insert missing verses (16:26–27)
* Delete the placeholder at 16:25

These actions would break alignment with the original source and introduce inconsistencies.

---

## Recommended Approach

Treat this as:

* A **structural variation**
* A **source-faithful representation**
* A **non-errata condition**

If needed, flag for awareness rather than correction.

---

## Summary

The relocation of the Romans doxology to **Romans 14:26** is:

> **Expected, valid, and correct for this dataset**

No action is required.
